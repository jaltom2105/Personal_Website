AI Empathy Modeling

Role

Independent Researcher

Period

Ongoing (2024)

Keywords

LLMs, Cognitive Empathy, Anthropomorphism

Project Overview

This ongoing research project investigates the communicative intent behind LLM-generated responses. Specifically, I am analyzing how "empathetic" phrasing in AI agents affects the psychological trust and cognitive offloading patterns of elderly users.

By testing various prompt architectures—ranging from purely functional to highly social—I am mapping the "Uncanny Valley" of AI empathy. The goal is to determine if an AI that simulates empathy actually improves user comprehension or if it creates a false sense of cognitive security.

Core Questions

  • Does linguistic mirroring in AI agents lead to higher user compliance in medical reminders?
  • How does the perception of "intent" change when the AI explicitly identifies its artificial nature?
  • Can we model "cognitive empathy" without relying on deceptive anthropomorphized traits?
